Character Design

My first approach of designing a character from scratch

ROLE Illustrative Designer
TIMELINE 3 months
TEAM Martin, Sebastian, Simon
PROCESS Sketching, Wireframing, Illustration, Finalization

OVERWIEW

"Hosbol Pogronop" is a character concept that merges fantasy elements with an original design, resulting in a unique and memorable figure. As a Myconid Monk with a Hermit background, Hosbol Pogronop embodies both the wisdom and tranquility of a secluded life, coupled with the martial discipline of a monk.

Concept and Inspiration

The inspiration for Hosbol Pogronop came from the fascinating world of fungi and the mysterious qualities they possess. The Myconid race provided the perfect canvas to explore themes of nature, isolation, and inner strength. By blending these aspects with the disciplined and peaceful nature of a monk, the character is both serene and formidable.

Design Process

The design process began with rough sketches to explore the overall silhouette and key features of the character. The mushroom cap, serving as both head and helmet, was a focal point, representing the character's connection to the earth and the mystical. The body was designed to resemble a sturdy yet flexible trunk, emphasizing the character's resilience. The addition of monk-like robes, interpreted as natural, layered growths, ties together the character's background and class.

Posing and Expression

To bring Hosbol Pogronop to life, various poses were developed to showcase the character's personality and abilities. From meditative stances to dynamic combat moves, each pose reflects the duality of calm and strength inherent in the character. These poses also highlight the flexibility and flow of the design, ensuring the character feels dynamic and alive.

Final Thoughts

Creating Hosbol Pogronop was a rewarding process, allowing for the exploration of unique character traits through visual design. The final character stands as a testament to the power of combining natural inspiration with imaginative storytelling, resulting in a figure that is both otherworldly and deeply connected to the natural world.
